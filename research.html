Quantum Dynamics

Chemical processes are governed by the fundamental rules of quantum mechanics. Simulating these systems accurately is a very challenging task owing to the exponential scaling of quantum dynamics with the number of particles — the curse of dimensionality. I develop and use various rigorous approximations and exact theory to follow the dynamics in these many-body chemical and physical processes.

Recent Work

DMRG study of Hyperuniform Spin Chains

Hyperuniformity is the suppression of long-range density fluctuations. Such materials often show exotic physical properties. I explored hyperuniform quantum spin chains and found there to be a variable number of phase transitions, that might motivate the design of novel magnetic materials. Read more on arXiv or Phys. Rev. B.

Augmented Propagator Tensor Network Path Integral

We use ideas from tensor network representations to simplify real time path integral calculations of a system coupled with a harmonic bath. Matrix product structures lead to enormous compressions that lead to an easily parallelizable, powerful simulation algorithm. Applications to charge and exciton transfer demonstrated. Read more on arXiv.

Multisite Tensor Network Path Integral

Extension of tensor network path integral framework to simulate extended systems coupled to local baths like exciton transfer and charge transfer chains coupled with local vibrations. Manuscript link will be uploaded soon.
